A New Value Of A System

Let's invest a new value, called the 'liveliness' of the system. This value can be found by:
\[Liveliness = Kenetic \; Energy - Potential \; Energy\]

  • A particle with a high ‘liveliness’ will have a high velocity, but it cannot go much faster.
  • A particle with low ‘liveliness’ will have a high velocity, but it can go faster.
  • A particle with negative ‘liveliness’ will have a low velocity, but it can go faster.

When \(a \ne 0\), there are two solutions to \(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\) and they are \[x = {-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac} \over 2a}.\]
