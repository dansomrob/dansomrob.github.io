Introduction

Let's invest a new value, called the 'liveliness' of the system. This value can be found by:
\[Liveliness = Kenetic \; Energy - Potential \; Energy\] A particle with a high ‘liveliness’ will have a high velocity, but it cannot go much faster. A particle with low ‘liveliness’ will have a high velocity, but it can go faster. A particle with negative ‘liveliness’ will have a low velocity, but it can go faster. The ‘liveliness’ of a system give us a rough understanding of the current velocity and potential future velocity.
