Introduction

Let's invest a new value, called the 'liveliness' of the system. This value can be found by:
\[Liveliness = Kenetic \; Energy - Potential \; Energy\] A particle with a high ‘liveliness’ will have a high velocity, but it cannot go much faster.
A particle with low ‘liveliness’ will have a high velocity, but it can go faster.
A particle with negative ‘liveliness’ will have a low velocity, but it can go faster.

 The ‘liveliness’ of a system give us a rough understanding of the current velocity and potential future velocity.
\[𝐿(𝑥,𝑣)=𝑇(𝑣)−𝑉(𝑥)\] Where L(x,v) is the ‘liveliness’, T(v) is the kinetic energy and V(x) is the potential energy.

And now replace the word 'liveliness' with lagrnagian and we can properly go exploring.
