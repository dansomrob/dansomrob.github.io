Cellular Automata

Cellular automata may be viewed as computers, in which data represented by initial
configurations is processed by time evolution.

Steven Wolfram

Cellular automata are simple mathematical models where we have a bunch of cells which can exist in \(k\) states.

Let's begin by looking at cellular automata when \(k=2\), here taking either a value of 0 or 1.

We can view 0 as being dead (white cell) and 1 and being alive (black cell).

Now this doesn't really do much. So let's add a time dimension (we're only going to look at one dimension automata for now). Lets us the following equation \[a_i^{t+1}=a_{i-1}^{t}+a_{i+1}^t\quad{\text{mod 2}}\] Here, \(t\) is the time dimension, \(t+1\) is the row beneath \(t\).

\(i\) represents the dimension of the cell, with \(t-1\) to the left and \(t+1\) to the right.

Also note that \(a\) represnt the value of that cell. In this example \(a, i, t\) are all discrete.
